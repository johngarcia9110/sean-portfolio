International Knowledge Transfer in Photovoltaic Market Diffusion

Research Project - Lund University

This research investigated how deployment knowledge and industry best practices can be transferred from global PV leaders into growing markets. The chosen case was Mexico, due to its strong growth potential and connectivity with existing markets such as the US and Germany.

Business Model Analysis of Tesla’s Powerwall Battery Storage Systems

Research Project - Lund University

Immediately following the 2015 announcement of Tesla’s battery storage system, our team investigated how the business model of Tesla would morph and respond to various market conditions.

Clean Production Opportunities for Lithium Ion Batteries

Research Project - Lund University

The objective of this work was to examine from a high-level production and design perspective and identify opportunities to improve lithium ion battery production. Preliminary findings indicate that lithium ion batteries are not designed for ‘manufacturability’ or ‘recyclability’ which can increase system costs. Other improvement opportunities include quality monitoring and modular design.

Proliferating Arizona Solar through the Hyperloop Project

Undergraduate Thesis - Arizona State University

This was a preliminary study to investigate the implications of the “Hyperloop” or a solar-powered high-speed rail project along the Arizona sun corridor. The research examined geographic routing considerations, market potential & grid-integration factors and identified key advantages of the Interstate-10 route.
